The School of Computing tracks Graduate student progress each semester to make sure the students are making satisfactory progress toward their degree.

I spent a lot of time on proper authentication, given that that is what this assignment is about. No Student should be able to see anything but their own forms; there is authentication checking for any /Advisor or /DGS pages (there are no DGS pages yet) and they also can't hack the URL to get to someone else's form and either view or change that. I implemented some simple JS on the Entrance portal to display a different 4th element of the navbar depending on the role (in case someone came back to that page, but they were already logged in) and which hides the Login form until they press the Login button. The navbar also changes on the Due Progress Form, depending on if an advisor or student go there.

There were several major bugs from previous versions that I had to fix as well. When a student edited their progress form, if they changed advisors, I didn't update the 'Student' table, which stores the most current advisor, and the form didn't take into account an existing Student going to that form and filling out a New Form, instead of editing an existing form.

Testing strategy was simple: Use OSX, Window 7, Windows 10, and Ubuntu Linux, along with their native browsers and extra browsers, like Chrome, and Firefox, to make sure the page renders correclty. Only Support for IE 11 and up, as Microsoft has terminated support for IE9 and 10.
